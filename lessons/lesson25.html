The LAST Method for Problem Resolution

üéØ LAST Method Framework

L
LISTEN - Give your full attention, maintain eye contact, let them finish speaking without interrupting
A
APOLOGIZE - Express genuine empathy: "I'm sorry this happened" or "I understand your frustration"
S
SOLVE - Offer practical solutions within your authority, explain what you can do
T
THANK - Thank them for bringing it to your attention and for their patience

üó£Ô∏è Professional Phrases to Use

Listening: "I can see this is important to you..." | "Help me understand..."

Apologizing: "I sincerely apologize for the inconvenience..." | "I understand how frustrating this must be..."

Solving: "Here's what I can do for you..." | "Let me see how I can help..."

Thanking: "Thank you for your patience..." | "I appreciate you bringing this to my attention..."

Practice Scenarios

Scenario 1: Meal Disappointment

Situation: A passenger ordered a vegetarian meal but received chicken. They're upset because they haven't eaten in 8 hours and the flight is 6 more hours.

Your Role: Apply the LAST method to resolve this situation.

LISTEN: "I can see you're really frustrated about not getting your vegetarian meal."
APOLOGIZE: "I sincerely apologize for this mix-up. I understand how concerning this is, especially on such a long flight."
SOLVE: "Let me check what vegetarian options we have available in our extra meals, and I'll also see if we have any substantial snacks I can bring you right away."
THANK: "Thank you for your patience while I sort this out for you."
Scenario 2: Entertainment System Failure

Situation: A passenger's entertainment screen is completely black on a 10-hour international flight. They're traveling with a young child who was counting on watching movies.

Your Role: Handle this complaint professionally.

LISTEN: "I understand this is especially difficult with your child on such a long flight."
APOLOGIZE: "I'm so sorry about the entertainment system. I know how important this is for keeping your little one comfortable."
SOLVE: "Let me try resetting the system first. If that doesn't work, I can see if we have any available seats with working systems, or I can bring some activity books and crayons for your child."
THANK: "Thank you for letting me know about this issue."
Scenario 3: Temperature Complaint

Situation: A passenger complains that their section is too cold and they can't sleep. Other passengers nearby seem comfortable.

Your Role: Address their comfort concern diplomatically.

LISTEN: "I can see you're having trouble getting comfortable because of the temperature."
APOLOGIZE: "I'm sorry you're feeling too cold. Everyone's comfort is important to us."
SOLVE: "I can bring you an extra blanket and pillow. I'll also check if we can make a slight adjustment to the cabin temperature, though I need to balance everyone's comfort."
THANK: "Thank you for letting me know. I want to make sure you can rest comfortably."
