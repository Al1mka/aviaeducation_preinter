Theory & Best Practices

⏱️ 15 minutes

Section Timer

👋

The Perfect Greeting

Eye Contact: Make genuine eye contact for 2-3 seconds
Smile: Warm, authentic smile that reaches your eyes
Posture: Stand tall, shoulders back, hands visible
Voice: Clear, welcoming tone at appropriate volume

🗣️

Standard Phrases

Morning: "Good morning! Welcome aboard!"
Afternoon: "Good afternoon! Welcome to [Airline]!"
Evening: "Good evening! We're delighted to have you!"
Universal: "Welcome aboard! We're happy you're flying with us today!"

🌍

Cultural Considerations

Eye Contact: Adjust based on cultural norms
Personal Space: Respect varying comfort levels
Greetings: Learn basic phrases in common languages
Gestures: Use universally positive body language

⚡

Efficiency Tips

Positioning: Stand where you can see and be seen
Multitasking: Observe while greeting
Flow Management: Keep passengers moving smoothly
Team Coordination: Work seamlessly with crew

💡 Key Principle

The first 30 seconds of passenger interaction set the tone for the entire flight experience. Your greeting should convey competence, warmth, and professionalism while making each passenger feel valued and secure.

Practical Exercises

Section Timer

15:00
🎭

Exercise 1: Mirror Practice

Duration: 3 minutes
Practice your greeting in front of a mirror or camera. Focus on facial expressions, posture, and hand positioning. Record yourself to identify areas for improvement.

🎯

Exercise 2: Voice Projection

Duration: 3 minutes
Practice projecting your voice clearly without shouting. Try different tones and volumes. Practice speaking over ambient aircraft noise.

👥

Exercise 3: Passenger Types

Duration: 5 minutes
Practice adapting your greeting for different passenger types: business travelers, families, elderly passengers, nervous flyers, and VIP guests.

⏰

Exercise 4: Speed & Quality

Duration: 4 minutes
Practice greeting 20 imaginary passengers in 2 minutes while maintaining quality and warmth. Focus on efficiency without rushing.

Section 4

Real-World Scenarios

⏱️ 10 minutes

Section Timer

10:00
🎬 Scenario 1: Delayed Flight

Situation: Passengers are boarding after a 2-hour delay. Many are frustrated and stressed.

Your Approach: Acknowledge the delay with empathy: "Good evening! Thank you for your patience with today's delay. We're excited to get you to your destination safely and comfortably."

🎬 Scenario 2: Nervous First-Time Flyer

Situation: A passenger appears anxious and mentions it's their first flight.

Your Approach: "Welcome aboard! I see this might be your first flight with us - you're in excellent hands today. Please let me know if you have any questions during boarding."

🎬 Scenario 3: VIP Passenger

Situation: A frequent flyer or premium passenger boards.

Your Approach: "Good morning, Mr. Johnson! Welcome back aboard. We're delighted to have you flying with us again today. Thank you for your continued loyalty."

🎬 Scenario 4: Large Family Group

Situation: A family with young children and multiple carry-on bags boards.

Your Approach: "Welcome aboard, everyone! What an exciting family adventure! Let me help you get settled. Children, are you ready for takeoff?"
