Pre-flight Briefing Process

1. Crew Introduction & Role Assignment

Introduce team members, confirm positions, and assign specific responsibilities for the flight.

2. Flight Information Review

Review route, weather, turbulence forecasts, and any operational considerations.

3. Safety & Emergency Procedures

Review emergency equipment locations, evacuation procedures, and crew coordination protocols.

4. Passenger Services Planning

Coordinate meal service, special requests, and passenger assistance requirements.

Practice Scenarios

Scenario 1: International Long-Haul Flight

Situation: 12-hour flight from New York to Tokyo, 280 passengers, including 15 children, 3 passengers with mobility assistance needs, and severe turbulence expected over the Pacific.

Key Briefing Points:
  • ‚Ä¢ Extended service coordination for multiple meal services
  • ‚Ä¢ Special attention protocols for unaccompanied minors
  • ‚Ä¢ Mobility assistance passenger seating and evacuation plans
  • ‚Ä¢ Turbulence safety procedures and crew positioning
  • ‚Ä¢ Rest rotation schedule for crew fatigue management

Scenario 2: Medical Emergency Preparation

Situation: Passenger manifest shows a passenger with severe allergies, another with a heart condition, and the flight is to a remote destination with limited medical facilities.

Key Briefing Points:
  • ‚Ä¢ Review medical equipment locations and usage
  • ‚Ä¢ Identify crew members with medical training
  • ‚Ä¢ Establish communication protocol with ground medical
  • ‚Ä¢ Plan for potential diversion airports
  • ‚Ä¢ Review allergy management and EpiPen procedures

Scenario 3: Difficult Weather Conditions

Situation: Departure delayed 3 hours due to storms, passengers are frustrated, connecting flights will be missed, and severe turbulence expected throughout the flight.

Key Briefing Points:
  • ‚Ä¢ Customer service strategy for frustrated passengers
  • ‚Ä¢ Modified service procedures for continuous turbulence
  • ‚Ä¢ Safety briefing emphasis on seatbelt compliance
  • ‚Ä¢ Crew safety positioning during service suspension
  • ‚Ä¢ Communication plan for updates and reassurance

Scenario 4: New Crew Member Integration

Situation: You're the senior flight attendant with two new crew members on their first international flight. One seems nervous and the other is overconfident.

Key Briefing Points:
  • ‚Ä¢ Detailed explanation of procedures for new crew
  • ‚Ä¢ Confidence-building techniques for nervous crew member
  • ‚Ä¢ Clear expectations and boundaries for overconfident crew
  • ‚Ä¢ Mentoring approach and support systems
  • ‚Ä¢ Extra safety checks and supervision protocols

Scenario 5: Security Threat Assessment

Situation: Security has flagged a passenger for additional monitoring. The flight is to a high-risk destination and you have VIP passengers on board requiring discrete protection.

Key Briefing Points:
  • ‚Ä¢ Discrete monitoring procedures and crew assignments
  • ‚Ä¢ Communication protocols with flight deck and ground security
  • ‚Ä¢ VIP passenger protection without drawing attention
  • ‚Ä¢ De-escalation techniques and conflict resolution
  • ‚Ä¢ Emergency response procedures for security incidents
