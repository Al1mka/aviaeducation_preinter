üö® Key Statistics

1 in 604 flights experiences a medical emergency

Most common: Fainting/syncope (37%), respiratory issues (12%), nausea/vomiting (9%)

Diversion rate: Only 7% of medical emergencies require flight diversion

üè• Common Medical Emergencies

1. Syncope (Fainting) - 37% of cases

Causes: Dehydration, low blood pressure, anxiety, standing too long

Signs: Dizziness, pale skin, sweating, loss of consciousness

Action: Lay passenger flat, elevate legs, loosen clothing, provide oxygen if needed

2. Respiratory Distress - 12% of cases

Causes: Asthma, COPD, anxiety, allergic reactions

Signs: Difficulty breathing, wheezing, chest tightness, blue lips

Action: Assist with inhaler, provide oxygen, keep passenger upright

3. Cardiac Events - 11% of cases

Types: Heart attack, arrhythmia, cardiac arrest

Signs: Chest pain, shortness of breath, sweating, nausea

Action: Call for medical professional, prepare AED, consider diversion

4. Gastrointestinal Issues - 9% of cases

Symptoms: Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain

Action: Provide sick bags, fluids, anti-nausea medication if available
